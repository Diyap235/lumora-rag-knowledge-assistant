SECTION 1: ABOUT NEXACORE TECHNOLOGIES
================================================================================

1.1 Company Overview
--------------------
NexaCore Technologies Pvt. Ltd. is a mid-sized enterprise software and IT
services company headquartered in Bengaluru, Karnataka, with offices in
Hyderabad, Mumbai, Pune, and Chennai. Founded in 2009, NexaCore has grown to
employ over 2,400 professionals across engineering, product, sales, operations,
and corporate functions.

Our core verticals include:
  - Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) Implementations
  - Cloud Migration and DevOps Services
  - Cybersecurity Consulting
  - Data Engineering and Analytics

1.2 Mission and Values
----------------------
Mission: To deliver transformative technology solutions that drive measurable
business outcomes for our clients while creating meaningful careers for our
people.

Core Values:
  - Integrity First: We act with honesty and transparency in all interactions.
  - Client Centricity: Client success is our success.
  - Innovation: We challenge the status quo constructively.
  - People First: Our employees are our greatest asset.
  - Accountability: We own our results — both successes and failures.

1.3 Organisational Structure
-----------------------------
NexaCore operates under a matrix organisational structure. Employees report
to a functional manager (e.g., Engineering Manager, Finance Manager) and may
also report to a project or account manager depending on active engagements.

The senior leadership team comprises the CEO, COO, CFO, CTO, VP of HR, and
Regional Heads. HR Business Partners (HRBPs) are assigned to each business
unit and serve as the first point of contact for employee queries.

SECTION 2: EMPLOYMENT CLASSIFICATION
================================================================================

2.1 Types of Employment
------------------------
NexaCore classifies employees into the following categories:

  a) Permanent Full-Time Employees: Hired on an indefinite basis, eligible for
     all benefits described in this handbook.

  b) Fixed-Term Contract Employees: Hired for a specific duration (typically
     6 months to 2 years). Benefits eligibility is limited as specified in the
     individual employment contract.

  c) Probationary Employees: All new hires serve a probationary period before
     confirmation. See Section 3.

  d) Interns and Trainees: Governed by a separate Internship Policy. Not
     covered by this handbook unless specifically stated.

  e) Consultants and Contractors: Engaged through third-party agreements. Not
     employees of NexaCore. Not covered by this handbook.

2.2 Role Bands
--------------
NexaCore uses a graded role band structure:

  Band 1 (B1): Analyst / Associate
  Band 2 (B2): Senior Analyst / Senior Associate
  Band 3 (B3): Consultant / Specialist
  Band 4 (B4): Senior Consultant / Lead
  Band 5 (B5): Manager
  Band 6 (B6): Senior Manager / Associate Director
  Band 7 (B7): Director
  Band 8 (B8): Vice President and above

Compensation, leave entitlements, travel allowances, and authority limits
vary by band. Refer to the applicable appendix or policy document for band-
specific details.

SECTION 3: PROBATION POLICY
================================================================================

3.1 Duration of Probation
--------------------------
All new permanent employees serve a mandatory probationary period of 90 days
(3 months) from their date of joining. During this period, either party may
terminate employment with a notice period of 15 days or payment in lieu.

Exception: Lateral hires at Band 6 and above may have their probation waived
at the discretion of the CHRO with written justification.

3.2 What Happens During Probation
-----------------------------------
During probation, the employee:
  - Is not eligible for annual leave (Earned Leave/Privilege Leave). They
    accrue leave but cannot encash or take it until confirmation.
  - Is eligible for Sick Leave (up to 5 days, not carried forward post-
    confirmation if unused).
  - Is not eligible for domestic or international travel reimbursement beyond
    standard commute.
  - May attend all company events and training programs.
  - Will be assigned a buddy/mentor for the first 30 days.
  - Will receive a 30-day and 60-day check-in with their manager.

3.3 Probation Review
---------------------
At the end of the 90-day period, the reporting manager submits a probation
evaluation form through the HRMS (NexaCore People Portal). The HRBP reviews
the form and initiates confirmation.

Possible outcomes:
  a) Confirmed: Employee transitions to permanent status. Full benefits activate.
  b) Extended Probation: Probation extended for a further 30 or 60 days with
     documented performance improvement expectations.
  c) Separation: Employment terminated. Notice and final settlement processed
     within 15 working days.

3.4 Extended Probation Rules
------------------------------
Probation can be extended a maximum of two times. The total probation period
shall not exceed 180 days. After 180 days, the employee must either be
confirmed or separated. Failure to initiate the review is an HR compliance
violation.

SECTION 4: WORKING HOURS AND ATTENDANCE
================================================================================

4.1 Standard Working Hours
----------------------------
NexaCore operates a 5-day work week, Monday through Friday.

  Standard Shift (default): 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, with a 1-hour lunch break.
  Flexible Window: Employees may start between 8:00 AM and 10:30 AM and end
  correspondingly, subject to manager approval.

A minimum of 8.5 hours (excluding lunch) constitutes a full working day.
Employees are expected to log a minimum of 42.5 hours per week.

4.2 Attendance Marking
-----------------------
Attendance is tracked through:
  - Biometric devices at office entry points, OR
  - Remote check-in via the NexaCore People Portal (for remote/hybrid employees)

Failure to mark attendance on more than 3 consecutive days without intimation
will be treated as unauthorised absence, which may lead to loss of pay (LOP)
and disciplinary action.

4.3 Late Arrivals and Early Departures
----------------------------------------
Up to 3 late arrivals (more than 30 minutes after shift start) per month are
condoned without penalty. The 4th and subsequent late arrivals in the same
calendar month result in a half-day LOP for each instance.

Early departures beyond the permissible flexible window follow the same rule.

4.4 Remote Work Policy
-----------------------
Employees at B3 and above may be eligible for up to 2 work-from-home days per
week, subject to:
  - Manager approval
  - Minimum 3 months of confirmed service
  - No active Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
  - Role not requiring mandatory on-site presence

Employees working remotely must be reachable during standard working hours
and must mark attendance via the portal. Failure to be reachable for 2+ hours
during core hours (10:00 AM to 4:00 PM) without prior intimation will be
treated as a half-day absence.

4.5 Overtime
-------------
NexaCore does not have a structured overtime payment policy for Band 3 and
above. Employees at B1 and B2 working more than 48 hours in a week may be
eligible for compensatory off (Comp-Off), approved by their manager and
recorded in HRMS within 7 days of the overtime worked.

SECTION 5: CODE OF CONDUCT
================================================================================

5.1 General Behaviour Standards
---------------------------------
All employees are expected to:
  - Maintain professional, respectful, and inclusive behaviour at all times.
  - Protect confidential business and client information.
  - Avoid conflicts of interest and disclose potential conflicts immediately
    to their manager or the Legal team.
  - Not engage in any form of workplace harassment, bullying, or discrimination.
  - Represent NexaCore's values in client-facing and public interactions.

5.2 Conflict of Interest
-------------------------
Employees must not:
  - Hold a financial interest in a competitor or client without disclosure.
  - Engage in secondary employment that conflicts with NexaCore's interests.
  - Accept gifts from vendors or clients valued over INR 2,000 per occasion.
    Gifts above this value must be declared to HR and Finance within 5 days.
  - Use company resources for personal commercial activities.

5.3 Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure
----------------------------------------
Upon joining, all employees sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) that remains
in force during and after employment. Disclosure of proprietary information,
client data, source code, pricing details, or business strategies to third
parties is strictly prohibited and may result in immediate termination and
legal action.

5.4 Social Media Policy
------------------------
Employees may not post content online that:
  - Discloses client names, project details, or internal financial data.
  - Makes disparaging remarks about NexaCore, its leadership, or employees.
  - Could be misrepresented as an official statement by NexaCore.

Personal opinions on industry topics are generally permitted provided they
include a clear disclaimer: "Views are personal and not those of my employer."

5.5 Dress Code
---------------
NexaCore follows a smart-casual dress code for office days. Formal attire is
expected for client-site visits, presentations, and formal events. Employees
must use judgement appropriate to the context. Overly casual clothing (e.g.,
torn jeans, slippers) is not permitted in the office.

SECTION 6: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

6.1 Performance Review Cycle
------------------------------
NexaCore follows a bi-annual performance review cycle:

  - Mid-Year Review: September (for the period April to September)
  - Annual Review: March (for the full financial year, April to March)

Performance is rated on a 5-point scale:
  5 – Exceptional
  4 – Exceeds Expectations
  3 – Meets Expectations
  2 – Partially Meets Expectations
  1 – Does Not Meet Expectations

6.2 Goal Setting
-----------------
At the beginning of each financial year (April), employees set goals in the
HRMS aligned to team and company OKRs. Goals are reviewed and approved by the
reporting manager. Mid-year goals may be revised if business needs change.

6.3 Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
----------------------------------------
Employees who receive a rating of 1 or 2 at the Annual Review may be placed
on a PIP. The PIP includes:
  - Specific, measurable improvement objectives with timelines.
  - Bi-weekly check-ins with the manager.
  - HR oversight throughout the process.
  - Duration: typically 60 to 90 days.

During an active PIP:
  - The employee is not eligible for promotion or salary increment.
  - Remote work privileges may be suspended.
  - Leave greater than 3 consecutive days requires HRBP approval.

An employee who successfully completes a PIP is confirmed back on regular
performance track. An employee who fails to meet PIP objectives may be
separated from the organisation with appropriate notice.

6.4 Promotion Policy
---------------------
Promotions are considered annually at the time of the Annual Review.
Requirements for promotion include:
  - Minimum 18 months in current role (exceptions possible for exceptional
    performers with VP-level endorsement).
  - A rating of 4 or 5 at the most recent Annual Review.
  - A documented business case submitted by the reporting manager.
  - Approval by the band's promotion committee.

SECTION 7: COMPENSATION AND PAYROLL
================================================================================

7.1 Salary Structure
---------------------
Employee compensation comprises:
  - Fixed Gross Salary (Basic + HRA + Conveyance + Special Allowance)
  - Variable Pay (performance-linked; applicable from B3 onwards)
  - Statutory benefits (PF, Gratuity, ESI as applicable)

The CTC (Cost to Company) breakup is provided in each employee's offer letter
and reflected in the payslip available on the NexaCore People Portal.

7.2 Payroll Processing
-----------------------
Salary is processed on the last working day of each calendar month. If the
last working day falls on a public holiday, salary is credited on the
preceding working day.

Salary is directly transferred to the bank account registered in the employee's
profile on the HR portal. Employees are responsible for keeping bank details
updated.

7.3 Tax and Declarations
-------------------------
NexaCore deducts TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) per the Income Tax Act.
Employees must submit their investment declarations by April 30 for the new
financial year. Final proof of investments must be submitted by January 31.
Failure to submit on time results in TDS deduction on maximum slab.

7.4 Salary Revisions
---------------------
Salary revisions occur annually in April based on performance ratings:
  Rating 5 – Up to 25% increment
  Rating 4 – 15% to 20% increment
  Rating 3 – 8% to 12% increment
  Rating 2 – 0% to 5% increment (discretionary)
  Rating 1 – No increment; PIP initiated

SECTION 9: BENEFITS AND PERQUISITES
================================================================================

9.1 Health Insurance
---------------------
All confirmed employees are enrolled in NexaCore's Group Mediclaim Policy:
  - Coverage: INR 5,00,000 per annum (floater for self + spouse + 2 children)
  - Coverage for parents: INR 3,00,000 per annum (optional; premium shared)
  - Pre-existing conditions covered from Day 1 for self and spouse.

Claims are managed through our TPA (Third-Party Administrator): StarHealth
Assist. For claim procedures, contact healthbenefits@nexacore.in.

9.2 Life and Accident Insurance
---------------------------------
  - Group Term Life Insurance: 4x annual CTC (for all confirmed employees)
  - Personal Accident Cover: 3x annual CTC
  - Both policies are company-funded.

9.3 Provident Fund (PF)
------------------------
All employees are enrolled in the Employee Provident Fund (EPF) under the
EPF Act, 1952. Employee contribution: 12% of Basic Salary. Company
contribution: 12% of Basic Salary (3.67% to EPF, 8.33% to EPS). The
Universal Account Number (UAN) is provided on the payslip.

9.4 Gratuity
-------------
Employees who complete 5 or more years of continuous service are eligible for
gratuity under the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972. Calculation: (Last Basic
Salary / 26) × 15 × Number of Years of Service.

9.5 Employee Assistance Programme (EAP)
-----------------------------------------
NexaCore provides a free, confidential Employee Assistance Programme covering
mental health counselling, financial advisory, and legal consultation. Contact
eap@nexacore.in or call 1800-XXX-XXXX (toll-free).

9.6 Learning and Development

SECTION 12: DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES
================================================================================

12.1 Principles
----------------
NexaCore follows a fair and progressive disciplinary process. The aim is
corrective, not punitive, except in cases of serious misconduct.

12.2 Types of Misconduct
-------------------------
Minor Misconduct (examples):
  - Repeated late attendance
  - Non-compliance with dress code
  - Failure to submit timesheets on time
  - Disrespectful communication

Major Misconduct (examples):
  - Harassment or discrimination
  - Falsification of expense claims
  - Disclosure of confidential information
  - Violence or threats in the workplace
  - Gross insubordination
  - Theft or fraud

12.3 Progressive Disciplinary Steps
--------------------------------------
For Minor Misconduct:
  Step 1: Verbal warning (documented in employee file)
  Step 2: Written warning
  Step 3: Final written warning + corrective action plan
  Step 4: Suspension (with or without pay, depending on severity)
  Step 5: Termination

For Major Misconduct:
  The above steps may be bypassed. Depending on severity, action may commence
  at Step 3, 4, or directly at termination, following a proper inquiry process.

12.4 Inquiry Process
---------------------
When a formal inquiry is required:
  1. HR issues a charge sheet to the employee.
  2. Employee has 48 hours to respond (or more, at HR discretion).
  3. An Inquiry Committee is constituted (HR + neutral manager + optional
     legal representative).
  4. The inquiry is conducted within 15 working days.
  5. The Inquiry Committee submits findings to the HR Head.
  6. The HR Head, in consultation with the relevant VP, decides on action.
  7. The employee is informed in writing within 5 working days of the decision.

The employee has the right to be heard, but not the right to legal
representation during the inquiry (unless the matter involves criminal
proceedings).

SECTION 13: GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
================================================================================

13.1 Informal Resolution
-------------------------
Employees are encouraged to first attempt informal resolution by discussing
the issue with their manager. If the manager is the subject of the grievance,
the employee should approach the HRBP directly.

13.2 Formal Grievance Procedure
---------------------------------
Step 1: Employee submits a written grievance to the HRBP via email at
        grievance@nexacore.in or through the portal. The grievance must be
        filed within 30 days of the incident.

Step 2: HRBP acknowledges the grievance within 3 working days and begins
        investigation.

Step 3: HRBP issues a written response within 15 working days.

Step 4: If unsatisfied, the employee may escalate to the HR Head within
        7 working days of receiving the response.

Step 5: If still unresolved, the matter may be escalated to the CHRO or
        submitted to the Employee Relations Committee.

13.3 Anti-Retaliation Policy
------------------------------
NexaCore strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who files a
grievance in good faith. Retaliation by a manager or colleague will be treated
as Major Misconduct. Employees who experience retaliation must report it
immediately to the CHRO.

13.4 POSH Committee (Prevention of Sexual Harassment)
-------------------------------------------------------
NexaCore has a constituted Internal Committee (IC) under the POSH Act, 2013.
Complaints of sexual harassment must be filed directly with the IC
Presiding Officer. The IC process is separate from the general grievance
process and governed by the POSH Policy (separate document). Contact:
posh@nexacore.in.

SECTION 14: SEPARATION AND EXIT PROCESS
================================================================================

14.1 Notice Period
-------------------
Standard notice periods by band:
  B1 – B2: 30 days
  B3 – B4: 45 days
  B5 – B6: 60 days
  B7 – B8: 90 days

Notice must be submitted in writing through the HRMS Exit Module. Verbal
resignation is not accepted. Notice period begins from the date of written
submission.

14.2 Notice Buy-Out
--------------------
Employees may request to buy out their notice period by paying the equivalent
of remaining notice period salary. This is subject to business approval from
the Delivery Head and HR. Payment must be made before the last working day.

14.3 Garden Leave
------------------
NexaCore reserves the right to place an employee on garden leave during the
notice period, whereby the employee is asked to not report to work while
remaining on the payroll.

14.4 Exit Formalities
----------------------
Before the last working day, the employee must complete:
  - Handover of all ongoing work and documentation (checklist in HRMS)
  - Return of company assets (laptop, ID card, access card)
  - Clearance from Finance, IT, and Admin
  - Exit interview (optional but encouraged)

Full and final settlement (F&F) will be processed within 30 working days
of the last working day, subject to clearances being completed.

14.5 Termination by Company
-----------------------------
In cases of termination for cause, the investigation and approval process
(Section 12) must be completed first. For termination without cause (e.g.,
role elimination), employees receive severance as per the applicable band:
  B1 – B4: 1 month CTC per year of service (capped at 6 months)
  B5 and above: Governed by individual contract.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS – EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK
================================================================================

Q1: I just joined. When will I be confirmed?
A: After your 90-day probation period, your manager initiates the confirmation
   process. If you don't hear anything by Day 85, proactively follow up with
   your manager and HRBP.

Q2: Can I moonlight or freelance while employed at NexaCore?
A: Light freelance work in a non-competing domain may be permissible with
   prior written disclosure to your manager and HRBP. Any work for a direct
   competitor or NexaCore's clients is strictly prohibited. Refer to Section 5.2.

Q3: What if I disagree with my performance rating?
A: You may raise a rating dispute through the HRMS within 7 days of receiving
   your final rating. The HRBP will facilitate a review discussion with you
   and your manager. If still unresolved, escalate per Section 13.

Q4: What is the process for applying for an internal transfer?
A: Internal transfer requests can be submitted on the portal after 18 months
   in the current role. The transfer is subject to the receiving team's
   headcount approval and the releasing manager's agreement.

Q5: Who do I contact for payslip or TDS certificate issues?
A: Contact payroll@nexacore.in for payslip corrections and tds@nexacore.in
   for Form 16 and TDS certificate queries.

Q6: How do I escalate an HR issue if my HRBP is not responsive?
A: If your HRBP does not respond within the SLA (3 working days for
   acknowledgement, 15 for resolution), escalate directly to the HR Head
   at hrhead@nexacore.in. You may also use the anonymous concerns hotline.
