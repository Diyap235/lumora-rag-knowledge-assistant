WELCOME MESSAGE

Congratulations on joining NexaCore Technologies! We are delighted to have you
with us and look forward to your contributions. Onboarding at NexaCore is
designed to be comprehensive, structured, and supportive. This guide will walk
you through everything you need to do in your first 90 days.

This guide should be read alongside:
  - Employee Handbook (Employee_Handbook.docx): Policies and conduct
  - Leave Policy (Leave_Policy.docx): Leave entitlements
  - IT Setup Guide (IT_Setup_Guide.docx): Technical setup
  - Reimbursement SOP (Reimbursement_SOP.docx): Expense claims

If at any point you feel confused or overwhelmed, your assigned Onboarding
Buddy and your HRBP are here to help.

SECTION 1: BEFORE YOUR FIRST DAY (PRE-JOINING CHECKLIST)
================================================================================

1.1 Actions Required from You (Before Day 1)
----------------------------------------------
Within 5 working days of receiving your offer letter:

  [ ] Sign and return the offer letter via email to hrops@nexacore.in.
  [ ] Complete the Pre-Joining Digital Form on the link provided by HR.
      This form collects:
        - Personal details (name, address, date of birth, PAN, Aadhaar)
        - Bank account details (for salary credit)
        - Emergency contact information
        - Educational qualifications
        - Previous employment history
        - UAN (Universal Account Number for PF transfer, if applicable)
  [ ] Arrange the following original documents for Day 1 submission:
      (Full list in Section 6)
  [ ] Confirm your joining date with HR if it needs to be adjusted.
  [ ] Read the Welcome Packet (shared via email after offer acceptance).
  [ ] Download the NexaCore People Portal app (iOS/Android) and register
      using your personal email (before corporate email is active).

1.2 What NexaCore Will Prepare for You
----------------------------------------
Before your first day, HR and IT will:
  - Create your corporate email account.
  - Configure your laptop and install standard software.
  - Add you to the company directory.
  - Assign you to your team's project channels in Slack.
  - Schedule your first-day orientation and manager introduction meeting.
  - Assign an Onboarding Buddy from your team.
  - Prepare a welcome kit (NexaCore branded merchandise; available at the
    HR desk on Day 1).

SECTION 2: DAY 1: ARRIVAL AND ADMINISTRATIVE SETUP
================================================================================

2.1 Arrival Protocol
---------------------
  - Report to the Reception Desk at your joining office location at 9:00 AM.
  - Carry a government-issued photo ID for building entry.
  - Inform security that you are a new joiner. They will direct you to HR.

2.2 First Hour Activities
--------------------------
  9:00 AM – 9:30 AM: Reception and HR Welcome
    - HR Operations Coordinator greets you.
    - You receive your temporary access card.
    - You are taken to the HR Bay.

  9:30 AM – 10:30 AM: Documentation Submission
    - Submit all original and photocopy documents to HR.
    - HR verifies and acknowledges receipt.
    - Sign the following:
        * Employment Agreement / Appointment Letter (if not pre-signed)
        * Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
        * Employee Handbook Acknowledgement Form
        * IT Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)
        * Conflict of Interest Declaration Form

  10:30 AM – 11:00 AM: IT Equipment Collection
    - Proceed to IT Help Desk (Floor 2, Building A).
    - Collect laptop, peripherals, and accessories.
    - Begin IT setup checklist (see IT_Setup_Guide.docx, Section 2).

  11:00 AM – 12:00 PM: HR Orientation (Group Session)
    - Overview of NexaCore, its structure, and culture.
    - Policies summary (handbook, leave, travel, IT).
    - Benefits and payroll overview.
    - Q&A with HRBP.

  12:00 PM – 1:00 PM: Lunch with Buddy
    - Your Onboarding Buddy will take you for a team lunch.
    - Informal introduction to team members.

  1:00 PM – 2:00 PM: Manager Meeting
    - Meet your reporting manager.
    - Overview of team structure, current projects, and immediate priorities.
    - Receive access to project documentation and handover (if applicable).

  2:00 PM – 5:00 PM: Self-Paced IT Setup
    - Complete the IT setup checklist.
    - Set up email, Slack, Jira, Confluence.
    - Complete the mandatory Day-1 Security Training.

  5:00 PM – 5:30 PM: End-of-Day Check-in with Buddy
    - Review what was completed.
    - Make a list of pending items.
    - Schedule Day-2 agenda.

SECTION 3: WEEK 1: ORIENTATION AND ACCESS SETUP
================================================================================

3.1 Monday to Wednesday Activities
------------------------------------
  - Complete all IT setup items (Section 2 of IT Setup Guide).
  - Complete mandatory compliance trainings:
      * Security Awareness Training (Day 1)
      * POSH Awareness Training (by Day 3)
      * Data Protection and Privacy Policy Training (by Day 5)
      * Code of Conduct Training (by Day 5)
    All training modules are at training.nexacore.in. Completion is tracked
    in the LMS. Incomplete mandatory training by the end of Week 1 will be
    flagged to your HRBP.

3.2 Thursday and Friday Activities
-------------------------------------
  - Shadow a team meeting or project stand-up.
  - Review all policies listed in this onboarding guide.
  - Meet key stakeholders (list provided by manager).
  - Review your 30-day goals (see Section 12).
  - Set up payroll preferences and tax declarations in People Portal.
  - Submit PF transfer form (if applicable; use UAN portal with company PF
    account number provided by HR).

3.3 End of Week 1 Check-In
----------------------------
  Friday of Week 1: 30-minute check-in with HRBP to:
  - Confirm all documents have been submitted and verified.
  - Confirm all accesses have been provisioned.
  - Address any open questions.

SECTION 4: MONTH 1: IMMERSION AND INTEGRATION
================================================================================

4.1 Project Induction
----------------------
By the end of Week 2, your manager will:
  - Formally assign you to a project (or client engagement).
  - Introduce you to the project lead and client-side contacts (if applicable).
  - Walk you through the project repository, architecture, and documentation.
  - Assign a starter task or initiative to help you build familiarity.

4.2 Department Orientation Sessions
--------------------------------------
HR schedules orientation sessions with key departments for all new joiners:
  - Finance and Payroll: Understanding your payslip and reimbursement process.
  - IT Security: Deep dive into data protection and device policies.
  - Legal and Compliance: NDA, IP assignment, and client confidentiality.
  - Learning and Development: NexaCore Academy and training resources.
  These sessions are held in the second and third week of each month for
  joiners of that month.

4.3 Goal Setting
-----------------
By Day 15, you must collaborate with your manager to set 30-day goals in the
HRMS. Goals must be:
  - Specific and measurable
  - Aligned to your team's OKRs
  - Achievable within 30 days, with stretch elements

The 30-day goal form is under HRMS > My Profile > Probation Goals.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: MONTH 2 AND 3: PROBATION PERIOD ACTIVITIES
================================================================================

5.1 30-Day Review
------------------
At Day 30 (±3 working days), your manager conducts the 30-Day Probation Review:
  - Discussion of 30-day goal achievement.
  - Feedback on performance and integration.
  - Any areas of concern raised and documented.
  - 60-day goals set.
  The review is recorded in HRMS by the manager within 2 days.

5.2 60-Day Review
------------------
At Day 60 (±3 working days), the 60-Day Review includes:
  - Review of 60-day goal achievement.
  - Discussion of career development aspirations.
  - Any emerging concerns or accommodation needs flagged.
  - 90-day goals set.

5.3 90-Day Confirmation Review
--------------------------------
At Day 85–90, the manager submits the Confirmation Review Form in HRMS.
The HRBP reviews the form and initiates the confirmation process.

If all is satisfactory, the employee receives:
  - A formal Confirmation Letter via email.
  - Full access to Earned Leave balance.
  - Eligibility for full travel reimbursement and company benefits.

For Extended Probation scenarios, see the Employee Handbook (Section 3).

SECTION 6: DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR ONBOARDING
================================================================================

6.1 Mandatory Documents (Must be Submitted on Day 1)
------------------------------------------------------
The following must be submitted as originals and photocopies (or notarised
copies where originals cannot be parted with):

  Personal Identity:
    [ ] PAN Card (original + photocopy) — MANDATORY for payroll; cannot
        join payroll without PAN
    [ ] Aadhaar Card (original + photocopy) — MANDATORY for identity verification
    [ ] Passport (photocopy, if available) — MANDATORY for international travel
        provisioning; must be submitted within 30 days if not on Day 1
    [ ] Voter ID or Driving Licence (any one; additional identity proof)

  Address Proof:
    [ ] Aadhaar Card or Passport — double-use as address proof
    [ ] Recent utility bill (within 3 months) if Aadhaar address is outdated

  Education:
    [ ] Class 10 (SSC) Marksheet and Certificate
    [ ] Class 12 (HSC) Marksheet and Certificate
    [ ] All undergraduate degree certificates and marksheets
    [ ] Postgraduate degree certificate and marksheets (if applicable)
    [ ] Professional certifications mentioned in resume (copies sufficient)

  Previous Employment:
    [ ] Experience letters / Relieving letters from all previous employers
    [ ] Last 3 months' payslips from most recent employer
    [ ] Form 16 from most recent employer (for TDS continuity)
    [ ] Increment letters (if CTC verification is required)

  Bank Details:
    [ ] Cancelled cheque OR bank passbook front page showing account number,
        IFSC, and name
    [ ] Account must be in the employee's name only (joint accounts not
        accepted for salary credit)

  Other:
    [ ] 2 passport-size photographs (coloured, white background)
    [ ] PF Transfer Form (FORM 11 / FORM 13) — if transferring previous PF

6.2 Documents Due Within 30 Days of Joining
---------------------------------------------
  [ ] Passport (if not submitted on Day 1)
  [ ] Marriage certificate (if applicable; for dependent enrolment in health
      insurance)
  [ ] Birth certificates of dependents (children) — for health insurance
  [ ] Disability certificate (if requesting accommodation under disability policy)

6.3 Consequences of Missing Documents
----------------------------------------
  - Missing PAN Card: Employee is added to payroll with maximum TDS deduction
    (30%) until PAN is provided. TDS is not refundable by NexaCore; claim
    refund directly from Income Tax Department.
  - Missing Experience Letter: Verification pending; probation confirmation may
    be deferred pending background check completion.
  - Missing Education Documents: Verification pending; offer may be rescinded
    if verified qualification differs materially from declared qualification.

6.4 Background Verification (BGV)
-----------------------------------
NexaCore engages a third-party Background Verification agency. The BGV process
is initiated on Day 1 and typically completes within 30 working days.

BGV covers:
  - Identity verification (PAN, Aadhaar)
  - Education verification (last degree)
  - Employment verification (last 2 employers)
  - Criminal record check
  - Global database check (for Band 5 and above)
  - Credit check (for Finance and Accounting roles)

If a BGV discrepancy is found:
  - HR raises it with the employee for explanation within 3 working days.
  - If the discrepancy is material (falsified qualification, unreported
    termination, criminal record relevant to the role), employment may be
    terminated even during or after probation.
  - Minor discrepancies (e.g., date difference, title mismatch) are
    investigated and documented.

SECTION 8: ONBOARDING FOR REMOTE EMPLOYEES
================================================================================

8.1 Remote Onboarding Differences
------------------------------------
If you are joining as a fully remote employee or working from a location
outside the headquarters city, the following apply:

  - Device shipping: Your laptop and peripherals are shipped to your home
    address 3–5 working days before Day 1. You must be available to receive
    the delivery. If you miss the delivery, contact IT immediately.
  - Documentation: Submit scanned copies of all documents via the secure HR
    document upload portal (link shared in welcome email). Original documents
    must be couriered to the HR Office (address in the welcome email) within
    10 days of joining.
  - Day-1 orientation: Conducted via Microsoft Teams. IT sends a Teams meeting
    invite to your personal email on the evening before Day 1.
  - Buddy: Assigned from the same functional team; regular video check-ins.
  - IT Support: All remote support via IT Service Desk portal or phone.

8.2 Home Office Setup Allowance
---------------------------------
Remote employees hired with a "Remote" designation are eligible for a one-time
Home Office Setup Allowance of INR 10,000 to purchase:
  - Desk, chair, lamp, or other ergonomic furniture
  - Internet equipment upgrades
This allowance is processed as a reimbursement through the Finance Portal after
submission of receipts. It is available once per employee and is taxable as a
perquisite.

SECTION 9: RELOCATION ASSISTANCE
================================================================================

If you are relocating to a new city for this role, the following assistance
is available (subject to the relocation clause in your offer letter):

9.1 NexaCore-Sponsored Relocation
------------------------------------
  - Packing and moving: Covered by approved movers. Contact admin@nexacore.in
    to engage the empaneled moving vendor.
  - Temporary accommodation: Up to 14 days at a company-empaneled hotel or
    serviced apartment (band limits apply; see Reimbursement_SOP.docx, Section 2.2).
  - City orientation: HR will connect you with a local employee ambassador
    who can help with housing, transport, and city logistics.

9.2 Relocation Bonus (if applicable)
--------------------------------------
Some offer letters include a Relocation Bonus (typically INR 25,000–75,000
depending on band and distance). This is:
  - Paid in the first month's salary.
  - Taxable as salary income.
  - Subject to a clawback: If the employee resigns within 12 months of joining,
    100% of the relocation bonus must be returned.
  - If the employee is asked to leave by NexaCore (other than for cause),
    no clawback applies.

SECTION 10: BUDDY PROGRAMME
================================================================================

10.1 About the Buddy Programme
--------------------------------
Each new joiner is assigned an Onboarding Buddy — a peer-level colleague
from the same or an adjacent team who has been with NexaCore for at least
12 months. The Buddy is not the same as the reporting manager.

10.2 Buddy Responsibilities
-----------------------------
  - Meet the new joiner on Day 1 (lunch and end-of-day check-in).
  - Be available for informal questions throughout the first 30 days.
  - Help the new joiner navigate office culture, systems, and norms.
  - Introduce the new joiner to the broader team and cross-functional contacts.
  - Provide a candid, friendly sounding board — not a formal evaluator.

10.3 New Joiner Responsibilities
----------------------------------
  - Actively reach out to the Buddy rather than waiting.
  - Come prepared with questions to each check-in.
  - Provide feedback on the Buddy experience via the HRMS survey at Day 30.

10.4 Duration
--------------
The Buddy relationship is formally active for 30 days but can continue
informally as long as both parties find it mutually beneficial.

SECTION 12: 30-60-90 DAY GOALS FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

12.1 Purpose
-------------
The 30-60-90 framework helps new employees structure their learning and
contribution arc during probation. Goals are collaborative — set by the
employee and manager together, with HRBP visibility.

12.2 30-Day Goals (Days 1–30)
-------------------------------
Focus: Learn and Absorb
Sample objectives:
  - Complete all mandatory compliance trainings.
  - Understand the team's current project portfolio, architecture, and toolchain.
  - Complete at least 1 starter task or code review/document review.
  - Meet all key stakeholders (internal and client-facing) in your area.
  - Identify 3 questions or improvement ideas about your domain.

12.3 60-Day Goals (Days 31–60)
--------------------------------
Focus: Contribute and Collaborate
Sample objectives:
  - Complete and deliver your first independently owned task/module.
  - Participate actively in at least 2 sprint cycles or project reviews.
  - Receive mid-way feedback from manager and address any gaps identified.
  - Establish a working relationship with at least 2 cross-functional
    colleagues (e.g., QA, PM, client contact).
  - Begin contributing to Confluence documentation for your project.

12.4 90-Day Goals (Days 61–90)
--------------------------------
Focus: Own and Grow
Sample objectives:
  - Independently manage a module, component, or client interaction.
  - Demonstrate understanding of NexaCore's delivery standards and quality bar.
  - Prepare and present a brief "onboarding insights" mini-deck to your manager
    (what you've learned, what surprised you, ideas you have).
  - Set your first-year performance goals in HRMS (to be ready for formal
    review post-confirmation).
  - Begin leveraging L&D budget for a relevant certification.

SECTION 13: CONFIRMATION AND POST-PROBATION
================================================================================

13.1 Confirmation Process
--------------------------
See Employee Handbook, Section 3.3. In summary:
  - Manager submits Confirmation Review Form in HRMS by Day 85–90.
  - HRBP reviews and approves.
  - Confirmation Letter is issued.
  - Employee transitions to full permanent status with all associated benefits.

13.2 Post-Confirmation Changes
--------------------------------
On confirmation:
  - Earned Leave balance becomes available for use.
  - Full travel reimbursement entitlements activate.
  - Eligibility for WFH days (up to 2 per week; subject to manager approval).
  - Corporate credit card eligibility (if Band 5+, auto-issued; Band 3–4,
    on request if travel frequency qualifies).
  - L&D budget fully activated.
  - Health insurance updated from provisional to confirmed employee.
  - PF contributions regularised (were provisioned from Day 1 but confirmation
    triggers formal enrolment letter).

13.3 First Annual Performance Review
--------------------------------------
For employees who join between January and September, their first performance
review is typically at the next Annual Review cycle (March). For employees
who join in October–December, the first formal review may be deferred to the
following March, with a mid-year check-in in September serving as the first
evaluation.

SECTION 14: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q1: What documents are required during onboarding?
A: Mandatory Day-1 documents include: PAN Card, Aadhaar Card, Class 10 and 12
   certificates, undergraduate degree, all previous employer experience/
   relieving letters, last 3 months' payslips, cancelled cheque, and 2 photos.
   Passport is required within 30 days (mandatory for international travel
   provisioning). Full list in Section 6.

Q2: I don't have my experience letter from a previous employer. What should I do?
A: Notify HR on Day 1. You may submit a self-declaration letter explaining the
   reason (e.g., employer shut down, document lost). HR will assess on a case-
   by-case basis. Provisional joining may be allowed, but background verification
   cannot be completed without it, which may delay confirmation.

Q3: When will my salary account be activated?
A: Bank account details submitted in the Pre-Joining Form are processed within
   the first week. If you join before the 20th of the month, you may receive
   a partial month's salary on the last working day. If after the 20th, you
   will be paid in the following month's cycle for the combined period.

Q4: Can I take leave in my first week?
A: You can take Sick Leave or Casual Leave (prorated balance available from
   Day 1). Earned Leave does not accrue until Month 2 and cannot be taken
   until confirmation. Taking leave in Week 1 without a strong reason is
   generally discouraged as it impacts the onboarding schedule.

Q5: What happens during probation if I perform poorly?
A: At the 30-day or 60-day review, your manager may flag performance concerns.
   A documented improvement plan is discussed. If performance does not improve
   by the 90-day review, probation may be extended (up to 2 times; maximum
   180 days total) or employment may be separated. Refer to Employee Handbook,
   Section 3.3.

Q6: I need access to a system that wasn't provisioned. What should I do?
A: Submit an access request via the IT Service Desk (servicedesk.nexacore.in).
   Your manager may need to approve it. If the system requires elevated
   privileges (e.g., production database, cloud admin), additional approvals
   from IT Head or CISO may be needed.

Q7: My Buddy is not responsive. What should I do?
A: Contact your HRBP directly. The HRBP will either re-engage the Buddy or
   assign you a new one. Do not let the programme lapse — the Buddy is an
   important resource during your first 30 days.

Q8: I relocated for this job. How do I claim the relocation allowance?
A: Submit receipts for packing, moving, and temporary accommodation through
   the Finance Portal under Relocation Expense category. Alternatively,
   contact admin@nexacore.in to use empaneled vendors who are billed directly.
   The relocation bonus (if applicable per your offer letter) is auto-credited
   in Month 1 salary. See Section 9 for full details.

Q9: I missed the POSH training deadline. What happens?
A: POSH training is mandatory. Missing it triggers an automated reminder,
   then HRBP follow-up. Complete it immediately. It cannot be waived.
   Continued non-completion is noted in your probation evaluation.

Q10: How do I know who my HRBP is?
A: Your HRBP's name and contact details are included in the welcome email from
   HR. They are also listed in the company directory under your department.
   You can also find your HRBP in the People Portal under My Team > HR Contact.
